Effective date: April 11, 2026

This Content Moderation Policy explains how mogged reviews uploaded photos, submissions, ratings, and repeated abuse patterns on `mogged.games`.

## Key points upfront

- mogged is designed to block or restrict unlawful, abusive, or high-risk content.
- We may use automated checks, provider-side checks, and manual review when needed.
- Read this page together with our [Acceptable Use Policy](/acceptable-use-policy), [Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy), and [AI Wrapper Disclaimer](/ai-wrapper-disclaimer).

## What moderation applies to

Moderation may apply to:

- text comparison submissions and instructions
- uploaded selfies, comparison photos, reference images, and related files
- battle ratings, results, and previews
- repeated account behavior that suggests policy evasion or abuse

## Prohibited content categories

mogged does not allow content involving:

- sexual exploitation, nudity meant for explicit use, or any sexualized minor content
- graphic violence, torture, self-harm promotion, or targeted threats
- hate speech, extremist ideology, or targeted discrimination
- fraud, impersonation, deceptive synthetic media, or harmful misinformation
- unlawful activity, trafficking, dangerous weapon use, or criminal instructions
- content that clearly violates intellectual-property or consent boundaries

## How moderation works

### 1. Prompt and upload checks

Before a battle runs, we may screen uploaded photos and comparison materials for policy violations or abuse signals. Unsafe or obviously disallowed requests may be blocked before they enter the workflow.

### 2. Output review

Battle results and ratings may be checked again after the comparison completes. If an output appears to violate policy, it may be withheld, discarded, or placed behind an account warning or review flow.

### 3. Abuse-pattern review

We may log repeated blocked attempts, refund abuse, evasion patterns, or account behavior that indicates misuse. These records are used for safety, fraud prevention, and operational protection.

### 4. Manual review when needed

Some borderline cases require human review. Manual review is used to resolve ambiguity, enforce policy consistently, and reduce false positives or repeated evasion.

## What happens when content is blocked

Depending on the case, mogged may:

- reject the prompt or upload
- suppress the resulting output
- warn the user
- place the account under review
- suspend or terminate access for repeated or serious violations

## Data handling

We try to keep moderation data limited to what is reasonably necessary for safety, abuse review, payment risk handling, and legal compliance. For general data-handling questions, see our [Privacy Policy](/privacy-policy).

## Appeals and support

If you believe moderation blocked a request incorrectly, contact <SupportEmailLink /> and include the affected account email, approximate time of the event, and a concise explanation of what you were trying to do.
