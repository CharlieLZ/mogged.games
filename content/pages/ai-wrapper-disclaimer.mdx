This AI Wrapper Disclaimer explains how mogged on `mogged.games` relates to upstream models, third-party providers, and the services sold on this site.

## Key points upfront

- mogged is an independently operated product and workflow layer.
- `mogged.games` is not the official website for any upstream model provider unless a page explicitly says otherwise.
- Read this page together with our [Mission](/mission), [Pricing](/pricing), and [Terms of Service](/terms-of-service) when you want the clearest service boundary.

## What mogged is

mogged provides its own public site, product positioning, billing surface, account system, task orchestration, browser tools, support flows, and policy layer. We package these parts into a workflow that is easier to use on `mogged.games`.

## What this site is not

Unless we explicitly say otherwise, mogged is not:

- an official upstream model vendor website
- an official subscription portal for a third-party model provider
- a general-purpose reseller that simply passes through another company's product without its own workflow, controls, or service layer

## How the wrapper boundary works

mogged may rely on external model, storage, analytics, or payment providers to deliver parts of the service. Even when upstream systems are involved, the product you buy from `mogged.games` is still our own hosted workflow layer, including:

- account and billing management
- battle submission and photo comparison UX
- moderation and abuse controls
- credits, subscriptions, and usage accounting
- browser-based preparation and cleanup tools
- support, refunds, and public policy pages

## References to third-party models and brands

We may refer to third-party models, architectures, or provider names to explain compatibility, workflow behavior, or output characteristics. Those references are descriptive only. They do not create sponsorship, endorsement, partnership, or agency by themselves.

Third-party trademarks, product names, and model names remain the property of their respective owners.

## Why this matters

This boundary is important because:

- the pricing on `mogged.games` describes this site's service, not a generic upstream subscription
- product availability, moderation behavior, and performance may depend on both our workflow layer and third-party systems
- support, refunds, and account enforcement should be read against our own policy pages, not inferred from another vendor's public docs
